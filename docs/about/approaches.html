Therapy Approaches

How CBT, psychodynamic therapy, and EMDR differ—and how I integrate them.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT focuses on the connection between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. It’s structured and goal-oriented, with practical skills like thought reframing, behavioral activation, and exposure. Helpful for anxiety, OCD, and depression.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Psychodynamic therapy explores patterns shaped by past experiences and relationships. By understanding what’s underneath current struggles, you can shift long-standing ways of relating to yourself and others. Helpful for self-esteem, relationship patterns, and life transitions.

EMDR

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) is an evidence-based therapy for trauma and distressing memories. Through bilateral stimulation, EMDR supports the brain’s natural healing process to reduce the intensity of traumatic memories and associated beliefs.

In our work, I draw on these approaches based on your goals. We’ll discuss options and choose together.
